Investigation Overview¶

In this slideshow, we will focus on analyzing user type v.s. duration relationship on the bike data.

Dataset Overview¶

Ford GoBike serves bicycle sharing service.
The data was collected from 2019-02-01 to 2019-02-28. There are 183,412 bike records in the dataset with 16 features.
The features we used in here are: user_type, duration_sec, member_birth_year, weekday(extracted from start_time)

User type: Subscriber v.s. Customer¶

Nearly 90% of the users are Subscriber, others are Customer.
Subscriber = Member or Customer = Casual

fig, ax = plt.subplots()
type_list = bike_nona['user_type'].unique()
for i, utype in np.ndenumerate(type_list):
    line_color = sb.color_palette()[i[0]]
    bike_type = bike_nona[ bike_nona['user_type']==utype ]
    year = np.sort(bike_type['member_birth_year'].unique())
    year_dur_mean = bike_type.groupby('member_birth_year').mean()['duration_sec']
    ax.errorbar(x=year, y=year_dur_mean, c=line_color, label=utype);
    ax.scatter(x=year, y=year_dur_mean);
ax.legend();
ax.set_xlabel("member_birth_year")
ax.set_ylabel("average duration_sec");
plt.title('Average Duration of Customer and Subscriber over member_birth_year');

User type v.s. Duration v.s. week_day¶

For Subscribers, their average durations are quite stable, with only a little increase on weekends.
For Customers, their average durations are much longer than Subsribers. Their weekend usage time also has large growth.

Recap¶

  1. Most bike records(90%) are from Subscriber, others are Customer.
  2. Bike record counts on weekdays are 1.5 more than weekends.
  3. Customer's bike usage time range is wider than Subscriber after log transformation.
  4. Bike average duration for Subscriber are quite stable, with only a little increase on weekends.
    For Customers, the average durations are much longer than Subsribers. Their weekend usage time also has large growth.
  5. Using member_birth_day in analysis, Subsciber's bike duration also shows lower than Customer's bike duration.
